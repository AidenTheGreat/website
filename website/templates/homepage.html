Our Company's Mission:

Increasing inflation, natural disasters, stagnant wages, and increased demand for living space in America, especially in large cities such as New York City, has caused a housing crisis. Helping Hands reaches out to those who are in need and connects them to organizations that can provide them with affordable housing in their preferred locations. Helping Hands also provides financial plans as well as other online resouces (government run websites and programs) to aid them in saving up for their housing.
Seek Out Information

Many that are homeless or in poverty stay in poverty because they are not aware of government/charitable programs that they could go to help for.

The Homeless Crisis in New York City (And Most of America)

According to the Coalition for the Homeless, in May 2021, there were 51,882 homeless people, including 15,930 homeless children, sleeping each night in the New York City municipal shelter system. A near-record 20,064 single adults slept in shelters in May 2021.

The Minimum Wage Problem

The nationwide minimum wage, which is currently at $7.25, is not enough to even rent a one bedroom apartment in 95% of America (averaging about $1,100 dollars per month), and that number is still increasing.

We Understand You

Helping Hands was founded by three New York City students who experienced or witnessed the widespread housing and poverty problem in New York City. We were lucky enough to find financial support programs, but now we want to give back and help others out of this situation.
